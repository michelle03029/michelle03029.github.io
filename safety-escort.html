UI/UX Design

CMU Safety Escort Redesign

Client: CMU Escort Bus Users

Designer: Michelle Chen

September - December, 2018



CMU Escort Bus Service is a transportation method monitored by the CMU Police to provide students with a way to get to school and back home. The CMU Escort Bus transportation app was made to track the location of the CMU Escort Bus in real time.

CMU Safety Escort Summary

The primary focus of the design of CMU Safety Escort is to safely transport CMU faculty and students off campus, usually back home With a mobile application, users will be able to help commuters easily find estimated arrival times and capacity of the bus.

Design Process

CMU Safety Escort Summary

The primary focus of the design of CMU Safety Escort is to safely transport CMU faculty and students off campus, usually back home With a mobile application, users will be able to help commuters easily find estimated arrival times and capacity of the bus.

Research

Secondary Research

To understand the current problem of why the escort bus service was not being utilized by students and faculty, I consulted the CMU Police, who manage the escort bus service to learn more about the escort bus service and also read about the service on the official website. Some key findings were:

- The escort bus drivers handwrite the estimated capacity of each stop but it is not shown in the application

- The escort bus takes requests of where to stop off campus (such as apartment complexes)

- The escort bus only operates from 6PM-6AM

Primary Research

After understanding more about the background of the escort bus system, I surveyed students and faculty to understand what they know about the escort system and their thoughts on how it can be improved.




From the research and primary model, I identified two opportunity areas that the escort bus service could focus on improving:

- Users & Time Management: Majority of users have time constraints that they operate on, whether it is going to CMU for a meeting or class or going back home from CMU.

- Users & Safety: Users may be traveling home at late hours by walking because they do not know how to use the bus or do not know how long it will take the bus to get to the bus stop.

Current State Model

A competitive analysis model was created to understand the current problem’s effects.



Ideation

Out of multiple concepts, three concepts were chosen and storyboards were created to take to the field for speed dating.

1. Ride-Share Program

2. Buddy System

3. Updated Transportation App

Feedback

- Ride-sharing relies heavily on CMU students with cars → what if there are not available students to drive in the program; Same with the buddy system

- Students need to go through the proper processes to obtain certifications/licenses to become a walker in the buddy system of ride-sharing program driver → can end up being a long process

- Does not take full advantage of resources that CMU already provides such as the already employed escort bus service drivers and the current application template

Revising the Concept

After receiving feedback from survey participants, I narrowed the solution to focus on transportation app modifications to take full advantage of the existing system. The reasons for this included:

- There are already safety protocols put in by CMU Police

- The existing system is more reliable than a student supported systems such as the buddy system and ride-sharing program








Final Design





Value Proposition

Users will be able to easily commute to and from campus with the escort bus application.

Bus Drivers will be able to simplify routes with user information from the application.

CMU Police will be able to reduce the number of incidents that CMU students or faculty encounter while walking alone at night.

Reflection

- By using the human-centered design approach, I was able to learn the importance of feedback and iteration to create a usable design to achieve end-user satisfaction
